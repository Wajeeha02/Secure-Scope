Threat intelligence sharing

Threat intelligence sharing is the practice of collecting, analyzing, and sharing information about cybersecurity threats and vulnerabilities among organizations, industry peers, and government agencies.By sharing threat intelligence, organizations can better protect themselves and their customers from cyber threats such as malware, phishing, and other cyber attacks. We provide a centralized platform for all organization to benefit from Cyber Threat Intelligence

Step 1: Collection of Threat Intelligence

In this step, we gather threat intelligence from various sources, such as internal security logs, open-source intelligence, and threat intelligence feeds. This helps build a comprehensive view of the threat landscape.

Step 2: Analysis and Classification

The collected threat intelligence is then analyzed and classified based on the type of threat, such as malware, phishing, or ransomware. This helps in identifying the severity and potential impact of the threats.

Step 3: Sharing of Threat Intelligence:

The classified threat intelligence is then shared with relevant stakeholders, such as industry peers, government agencies, or internal security teams. This helps in proactive threat detection and response.

Step 4: Ongoing Monitoring and Refinement

Finally, the threat intelligence sharing service needs to be monitored and refined continuously. This includes updating the threat intelligence sources, reviewing the effectiveness of the classification and sharing process, and adjusting the service to meet the changing threat landscape.
